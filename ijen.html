About This Tour

Kawah Ijen is one of the most extraordinary natural phenomena on Earth. Deep inside an active volcano in East Java, liquid sulfur burns at the rim of an acidic crater lake — producing an otherworldly electric blue flame visible only in darkness. To see it, you hike in at 1am. It is absolutely worth it.

At dawn, as the blue fire fades and the sky brightens, the crater reveals its second secret: a vast, impossibly turquoise lake, the world's largest acidic crater lake, wreathed in white sulfur clouds. Sulfur miners work here daily, a sight both humbling and extraordinary.

We drive you from your Bali hotel, stay overnight near the trailhead, and hike with you through the dark to witness something most people never see. Gas masks are provided for the sulfur — this is an adventure, not just a tour.

2-Day Itinerary

Day 1 — 14:00: Hotel Pickup in Bali

Your driver picks you up from your hotel and begins the drive east across Bali, crossing to Java via the Ketapang ferry at Gilimanuk.

Day 1 — ~20:00: Arrive at Guesthouse (Bondowoso)

Check in to a clean, simple guesthouse near the Ijen trailhead. Dinner is available locally. We recommend sleeping early — the next start time is midnight.

Day 2 — 00:00: Midnight Departure to Trailhead

Drive to Paltuding base camp. Your driver accompanies you on the hike (or guides you with a local ranger). Gas masks are provided.

Day 2 — 01:30: Blue Fire Viewpoint

Descend into the crater under the stars and witness the mesmerising blue sulfuric flames. A truly once-in-a-lifetime sight in a completely alien landscape.

Day 2 — 04:30: Sunrise Over the Crater Lake

As dawn breaks, the lake transforms from shadow into an impossible turquoise. Watch the mist roll in and the miners begin their extraordinary daily work.

Day 2 — 07:00: Descend & Return to Guesthouse

Hike back down, shower, change and have breakfast before the long but scenic drive back to Bali.

Day 2 — ~18:00: Arrive Back in Bali

Drop-off at your hotel. Tired, but completely transformed.
